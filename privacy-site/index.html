Privacy Policy

My Gallery is a local-first photo notebook. This policy explains what information the app uses, what stays on your device, and what happens when you choose to share content.

Last updated: 27 April 2026
Version line: 1.1.x

What My Gallery Does

My Gallery lets you sign in with Google, add photos from your camera or library, write or dictate captions, add tags and favorites, search saved items, and share selected images to other apps.

Information We Use

When you sign in with Google, the app may access:

  • Your name
  • Your email address
  • Your profile photo

This is used only to show your signed-in account inside the app.

What Stays On Your Device

This version of My Gallery stores the following data locally on your device:

  • Saved image copies
  • Captions
  • Tags
  • Favorite state
  • Theme preference
  • Signed-in profile state

My Gallery does not upload your saved gallery content to a custom backend in this version of the app.

Permissions

Permissions are requested only when you use the related feature:

  • Camera permission to capture a new image
  • Photo library permission to choose an existing image
  • Microphone permission to dictate a caption
  • Speech recognition permission to convert voice into text

Sharing

When you tap Share, the selected image is passed to the destination app you choose. That destination app handles the shared content under its own policies and practices.

Data Retention

Gallery data stays on your device until you delete items, clear the local gallery in Settings, or uninstall the app.

Third-Party Services

My Gallery uses Google Sign-In for authentication. Google may process account information according to Google’s own terms and privacy policies.
